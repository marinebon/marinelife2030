Marine Life 2030 is implemented utilizing a bottom-up approach which relies on volunteers from all around the world. A series of learning communities have been established, focused on:

- Best Practices and Interoperability
- Co-design and Stakeholder Engagement
- Capacity Development Citizen Science
- Early Career Ocean Professionals (ECOPS)
- External Communications and Outreach
- Indigenous Knowledge

More information about the program is available [here](https://drive.google.com/file/d/13CszfRpt91_kLflGsjyEJblCeLjTiA96/view) and [here](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1eRC0v1manpQoSgcKphfNycFIVg1GYCJz/view).

If you are interested in affiliation with Marine Life 2030, please seek endorsement by the UN Ocean Decade requesting such affiliation (visit the UN Ocean Decade [website](https://forum.oceandecade.org/page/take-action) to learn more). Please also review the Principles and Criteria for Marine Life 2030-affiliated Projects and Partners [here](https://marinelife2030.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/ML2030-Principles-Criteria-for-Projects-and-Partners.docx.pdf).

The Marine Biodiversity Observation Network (MBON) calls on nations, scientists, sponsors, and stakeholders to work together as a community of practice to support Marine Life 2030 to enable a dialogue, and coordination and cooperation between programmes. Please see this [Dear Colleague Letter](https://marinelife2030.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Dear-Colleague-Letter.pdf) and join the MBON/Marine Life 2030 Community of Practice ([GEO BON Members](https://members.geobon.org/pages/index)).

Email us at [marinelife2030@gmail.com](mailto:marinelife2030@gmail.com) with questions or for more information.
